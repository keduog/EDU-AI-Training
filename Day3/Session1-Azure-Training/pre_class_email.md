# Pre-class email — send 2 days before Day 3

Copy the text below, fill in the two placeholders, and send it to all trainees.

Sending this early is the single biggest time-saver on the day. If everybody has
accepted before class, Session 1 takes 20 minutes instead of an hour.

---

**Subject:** Day 3 (Azure) — an invitation is coming to your email, please accept it

---

Dear all,

On **Day 3** we work on Microsoft Azure. Two important points:

**1. You do NOT need to create an Azure account.**
You do not need a credit card, and there is no student eligibility check. You will use
the University of Gondar's subscription. Everything is already paid for.

**2. You WILL receive an invitation email — please accept it before class.**

- It comes from **Microsoft Invitations** (`invites@microsoft.com`)
- The subject looks like *"You're invited to the University of Gondar organization"*
- **Please check your Spam or Junk folder** — it very often lands there
- Click **Accept invitation**, then sign in
- If your email address does not already have a Microsoft account, Microsoft will help
  you create one for that same address. It takes about a minute and costs nothing.
- Any email works: Gmail, Yahoo, Outlook, or your `uog.edu.et` address

**After accepting, please do one quick check:**

1. Go to <https://portal.azure.com>
2. Sign in with the same email
3. Look at the **top-right corner** — it should say **UNIVERSITY OF GONDAR**
4. If it does not, click your name → **Switch directory** → **UNIVERSITY OF GONDAR**

That is all. If you can see the Azure portal, you are ready.

**If anything does not work**, do not worry and do not spend a long time on it — just
reply to this email and tell me:
- the email address you are using
- what the screen says

I will fix it before the session starts.

**Which email address will I use to invite you?**
I will send the invitation to the address I have for you. If you would prefer a
different one, reply and tell me which.

See you on Day 3.

*[Your name]*
*[Your contact]*

---

## Trainer notes on this email

- Send it **2 days before**, not the night before — some people need time to create a
  Microsoft account.
- Send a short reminder the evening before to anyone who has not accepted. Run
  `verify_setup.sh` to see who that is.
- Ask for preferred email addresses **before** you run `setup_students.sh`. Changing an
  address afterwards means inviting again and reassigning the role.
- The "check Spam/Junk" line matters more than anything else in this email. Most missing
  invitations are sitting in a junk folder.
